During the reign of the Almohads and the Hafsids, from the 12th to the 16th century, Tunis was considered one of the most important and richest cities in the Islamic world. Situated in a fertile plain region, in the north-east of Tunisia and a few kilometres from the sea, the Medina of Tunis is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the medina of Tunis is nowadays the capital of several dynasties with universal radiation, it represents a human establishment witnessing the interaction between architecture, urban planning and the socio-cultural and economic effects of previous cultures.

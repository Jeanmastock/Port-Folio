Around

St Vincent De Paul Cathedral

The cathedral replaces the city’s oldest Christian monument, the chapel built by Father Jean Le Vacher in 1650, whose altar was surmounted by a statue of Blessed Antoine Neyrot. Around the chapel was a Christian cemetery, one of the oldest in Tunis.

Zitouna Mosque

The Zitouna Mosque is the main mosque in the medina of Tunis. Attached to the malekism, it is the oldest and largest sanctuary of the capital of Tunisia. Built on an area of about 5,000 m2, the mosque has nine entrances and 184 ancient columns, mainly from the site of Carthage.

Mosque of the Kasbah

Built from 1231 to 1235 by the architect Ali ibn Mohamed ibn Kacem, on the orders of the founder of the Hafsid dynasty Abû Zakariyâ Yahyâ (reign from 1230 to 1249). It is the first mosque to be built after the Zitouna mosque, given that it was not permissible to have more than one mosque where the Friday sermon was given.

National museum of Bardo

The Bardo National Museum is a museum in Tunis, the capital of Tunisia, located in the outskirts of Bardo. It is one of the most important museums of the Mediterranean basin and the second museum of the African continent after the Egyptian museum of Cairo by the richness of its collections.

Museum of Carthage

The National Museum of Carthage is an archaeological museum located on Byrsa Hill in the heart of the city of Carthage in Tunisia. It is one of the two main archaeological museums of Tunisia with the National Museum of Bardo. Located near the Cathedral of Saint-Louis de Carthage, in the premises formerly occupied by the White Fathers, it allows the visitor to realize the magnitude of what the city’s facilities were in the Punic and later Roman times.

Oceanographic museum of Tunis

The Salammbo Oceanographic Museum, also known as Dar El Hout in Arabic, is the main Tunisian museum dedicated to the sea. It is located in Carthage, in the northern suburb of Tunis, on the site of its former Punic port. It was founded in 1924 as the Salammbô Oceanographic Station, and is then an outlet of the Monaco Oceanographic Museum.

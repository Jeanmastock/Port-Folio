Couscous is a traditional and unavoidable dish of the Maghreb but has some peculiarities in that made in Tunis. The recipe for Tunisian couscous is traditionally served with vegetables, chicken or red meat. A friendly and family-friendly dish filled with the city’s warm flavours, especially if you add the famous Harissa sauce and green peppers.

The Brick is an inevitable culinary delight in Tunis. Often made with tuna, the dough is composed of a mixture, half flour, half fine wheat meal, warm water and salt. The whole is kneaded until a paste is almost liquid (less liquid than a pancake paste) left at rest for two to three hours. The dough is again kneaded and brushed on a hot baking sheet or skillet.

Also known as Tunisian ratatouille, kafteji is a dish composed mainly of vegetables, spicy and spicy. The vegetables are fried one by one, then mixed together and seasoned with Harissa sauce. This dish can be used as a garnish of grilled meat or merguez. Here, it goes wonderfully well with a shoulder of lamb confit on the barbecue.

Marqa (literally “stew”) is a classic of Tunisian cuisine, and can be made with several ingredients. There is marqa batata (potato stew), marqa jelbana (pea/chicken stew), the one in the photo, or marqa glarha (squash stew). It is usually cooked with potatoes and simmered in a hot sauce containing peppers and peppers and then eaten with patties.

Whether you’re in Tunis, you’ll find a tasty grilled pepper salad. This salad is the delicious Slata mechouia, composed of tomatoes, roasted and chopped peppers after cooking, seasoned with salt, garlic, cilantro powder and caraway and sprinkled with olive oil. A dish that can be enjoyed with a patty or bread to highlight all the nuances of its flavors.

The Zlabia is an inevitable pastry shop passing through Tunis, and adored by Tunisian Tunisians but also by tourists who make it the most popular of the city. Zlabia is a fried confectionery made from sugar, flour and honey and then dipped in sweet oil. A confectionery that will do your taste buds good!
